--- layout: post title: Funny But Not Nice ---

On this Mother’s Day, I’d like to thank my mom for a small piece of genius she employed in raising me. This has nothing to do with band-aids, macaroni and cheese or any of the other daily gifts normally associated with having a mother. It concerns these words: 

“That’s funny but not nice.”

I have clear memories of my mother saying this to me, usually through laughter or at least with a wide smile. It meant that we’d just shared something in the course of talking that might have been hilarious, but that perhaps it shouldn’t leave the dinner table. Or the car. Or wherever we were having one of our great conversations. Maybe it was a lighthearted joke at someone else’s expense, perhaps it was a little dark, or likely it was just would have seemed strange coming out of the average eight-year-old’s mouth.

Regardless, it was genius because it allowed us to share in the inevitable things that make one giggle while feeling a little guilty as well. It made me feel like an adult for a moment, but thanks to the “FBNN” explanation I knew not to take the joke too seriously or share it in mixed company.

As an adult, I find that the concept of Funny But Not Nice comes in handy a lot. Maybe I’m crazy, but I feel like the culture my kids are bathing in is increasingly snark-based. Have you watched TV lately? It’s not so much “funny but not nice” but rather “cacklingly hilarious and let’s kick ‘em when they’re down.” I would love it if my kids grew up thinking that they had more to contribute than just throwing back their heads to laugh at the expense of others.

But I’d be a hypocrite if I said there wasn’t humor to be had in Funny But Not Nice; it just doesn’t have to define you. As with most things, a balance can be struck…and that is what my mom did for me. Thanks, mom. :)
